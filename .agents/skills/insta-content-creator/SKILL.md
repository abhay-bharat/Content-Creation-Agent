# Instagram Content Creator for AIThinkingLab

## What This Skill Does

Transforms rough ideas into complete, production-ready Instagram content that aligns with your AIThinkingLab brand. It:

- **Validates** ideas against your 5 locked content pillars (Experiments, Mental Models, Systems Thinking, Learning Notes, Meta/Process)
- **Generates** trending AI/ML topics relevant to your audience
- **Structures** content end-to-end: idea → hook → outline → script → visual direction
- **Checks** quality before production (usefulness, hooks, clarity, audience fit)
- **Recommends** the best format (reel, carousel, story, series post)
- **Plans** CTAs aligned with your metrics (saves, shares, follows)

## When to Use

- **New content idea**: "I want to explain how RAG systems work"
- **Trending topic**: "What should I cover about the new Claude update?"
- **Content block**: "Help me find 3 strong ideas this week"
- **Performance analysis**: "Why did this post underperform?"
- **Series planning**: "What should be in my next Mental Model Monday?"

## Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Idea to Content (Primary)

Use this when you have an idea and need a complete content plan.

#### Step 1: Validate Against Brand

Before investing production time, confirm the idea aligns with AIThinkingLab:

**Check against pillars:**
1. **Experiments** - Hands-on demos, tools, prompts, measurable outcomes?
2. **Mental Models** - Complex concept simplified? Intuitive explanation? Diagram potential?
3. **Systems Thinking** - Architecture, pipelines, tradeoffs, how real systems work?
4. **Learning Notes** - Clean summary of paper/lesson? Misconception correction?
5. **Meta/Process** - Creator systems, learning journey, public building (use sparingly)?

**Decision filter**: *Does this help someone think better about AI?*
- YES → Continue to Step 2
- NO → Refine or discard

#### Step 2: Choose Format

Match your idea to the most effective format:

| Format | Best For | Time | Frequency |
|--------|----------|------|-----------|
| **Reel** (20-35s) | Hooks, concepts, fast tutorials, news reactions, mental models | 2-3 hours | 2/week |
| **Carousel** (5-10 slides) | Deeper teaching, frameworks, comparisons, save-worthy content | 3-4 hours | 1/week |
| **Story** (6-9 frames) | Daily signals, behind-the-scenes, polls, quick thoughts | 30 mins | Daily |
| **Series Post** (text + image) | AI Signals (news), Mental Model Monday, Build Log | 2 hours | 3-5 AI Signals/week, series 1/week |

**Quick selector:**
- Breaking AI news or trend? → **AI Signals Reel** or **Carousel**
- Explaining hard concept? → **Carousel** or **Mental Model Reel**
- Showing experiment/failure? → **Build Log** or **Reel**
- Daily AI signals? → **Stories** + **AI Signals Posts**

#### Step 3: Develop Hook (Most Critical)

Your hook determines reach. Use one of three hook types:

**1. Surprise Hook**
- Counterintuitive insight: "What you think about RAG is probably wrong"
- Stat shock: "88% of AI projects fail because of this"
- Capability reveal: "Claude can now do this in one prompt"

**2. Clarify Hook**
- "What X actually means (not what people think)"
- "3 misconceptions about transformers"
- "Why your RAG system is slow (and how to fix it)"

**3. Opportunity Hook**
- "How engineers are using this to ship faster"
- "The one framework every AI engineer needs"
- "Here's the mental model that changed my code"

**Hook test**: Does it stop scrolling? Would an engineer save/share it?

#### Step 4: Create Core Content Outline

Structure depends on format:

**Reel Structure (20-35s):**
1. Hook (3-5 sec) - Stop scrolling
2. Setup (5 sec) - What are we talking about?
3. Core insight (8-10 sec) - The meat
4. Why it matters (5 sec) - So what?
5. CTA (2-3 sec) - Save/Share/Follow

**Carousel Structure (5-10 slides):**
1. Hook slide - Stop, preview, intrigue
2-4. Teaching (one idea per slide)
5. Proof/Example (diagram, code, real case)
6-8. Framework/Takeaway (if applicable)
9. Deeper resource link
10. CTA slide

**AI Signals Structure (5 slides - LOCKED):**
1. Hook - "OpenAI just released X"
2. What Happened - The facts (2 slides)
3. Why It Matters - Connections to AI landscape
4. ThinkingLab Take - Your analysis (engineer POV)
5. Source - Link + credibility

#### Step 5: Script & Write

Write as if teaching engineer-to-engineer:

- **Be specific** - Numbers, names, technical accuracy
- **No fluff** - Respect audience intelligence
- **Active voice** - "Transformers use attention" not "Attention is used"
- **Examples first** - Concrete before abstract
- **Human voice** - Contractions, short sentences, personality

**Reel script example** (convert to visuals + voiceover):
```
[Hook] "Your RAG system is probably broken"
[Setup] "Here's why vector search alone fails"
[Core] "You need: 1) Better chunking 2) Semantic routing 3) Quality evals"
[Why] "80% of RAG failures are data quality, not the model"
[CTA] "Save this and test your pipeline this week"
```

#### Step 6: Visual Direction

Apply AIThinkingLab visual system:

- **Style**: Minimal tech aesthetic, dark/neutral base, strong contrast
- **Frame principles**: One idea per frame, large readable text, clear hierarchy in <1 sec
- **Motion**: Supports meaning (not decorative)
- **Color**: Neutral base + accent (define 1-2 accent colors)
- **Typography**: 2-3 font sizes max, high contrast

**Tools**: Canva (templates), InVideo AI (reels), Figma (complex layouts)

**Template strategy**: Build 3-5 reusable templates, vary text/data/examples, save iteration time.

#### Step 7: Quality Checklist

Before production, ask:

- [ ] **Useful?** Would an engineer save/reference this?
- [ ] **Hook strong?** Does it stop scrolling? (Test on yourself)
- [ ] **Technically accurate?** No oversimplification errors?
- [ ] **Visually clean?** Hierarchy clear in <1 sec?
- [ ] **Sounds human?** Not robotic or overstated?
- [ ] **Right CTA?** Does action match format? (Save/Share/Follow/Comment)
- [ ] **Brand fit?** Aligns with curious, analytical, builder personality?

#### Step 8: Production & Distribution

- **Reel**: Script → storyboard → record/design → add audio → Canva export
- **Carousel**: Outline → script each slide → design in Canva → add captions
- **Story**: Quick record or Canva frames → publish daily at 8am/3pm
- **AI Signals**: Copy → design + source → publish daily

**Publishing cadence**: 
- 2 Reels/week (Mon, Thu)
- 1 Carousel/week (Wed)
- 3-5 AI Signals/week (Mon-Fri)
- 1 Daily Story (morning)

#### Step 9: Analyze & Iterate

Track these metrics:

- **Primary**: Saves, Shares, Follows per 1k views, Retention (% watching to end)
- **Secondary**: Likes, Reach
- **Goal**: 5% saves rate = content working

**Weekly review**:
- Which 2 formats earned highest saves?
- What hook type resonated?
- What topics generated comments?
- Double down on winners next week

### Workflow 2: Discover Trending Ideas (Secondary)

Use when you need fresh content ideas aligned with AIThinkingLab.

#### Step 1: Scan Trend Sources

Check these daily/weekly:

**Daily**:
- [X/Twitter AI trending](https://twitter.com/search?q=AI&src=trend_click) - Filter by engineers following
- [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/) - Top AI comments
- [Reddit r/MachineLearning](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/) - Latest papers/discussions

**Weekly**:
- [Hugging Face Blog](https://huggingface.co/blog)
- [OpenAI/Anthropic releases](https://openai.com/news/)
- [arXiv top papers](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent) - Sort by category
- AI newsletters you follow

**Monthly**:
- Conferences (NeurIPS, ICLR, CVPR summaries)
- Major tech blogs (Google AI, Meta AI)
- YouTube technical channels

#### Step 2: Filter for Your Audience

Ask for each trend/news:

- **Relevance**: Helps engineers? Builders? AI learners? (Not for general public hype)
- **Depth**: Can you explain the *why*, not just the *what*?
- **Timing**: News cycle or timeless? (80/20 rule: 80% timeless, 20% timely)
- **Angle**: What's your engineer POV? What do people get wrong?
- **Format fit**: Reel? Carousel? AI Signals? Build Log?

#### Step 3: Generate Your Angle

Take trending topic + your POV:

**Formula**: [Trend] + [Engineer lens] + [Hook type] = Idea

**Examples**:
- "GPTs launch" + "how they compare to function calling" + Clarify = Carousel
- "Prompt injection attacks" + "why evals miss them" + Surprise = Reel
- "New embedding model" + "when it matters vs legacy" + Opportunity = AI Signals

#### Step 4: Create Idea List

Use [reference/idea-capture-template.md](./references/idea-capture-template.md):

- **Topic**: Clear, 5 words max
- **Hook**: Your hook line
- **Pillar**: Which content pillar?
- **Format**: Reel/Carousel/Story/Series
- **Angle**: Your unique take
- **Effort**: 1-3 hours? Quick or complex?
- **Priority**: High/Medium/Low (based on audience fit)

Create 15 ideas weekly, select top 5, script top 3, produce 2-3.

## Content Pillars (Reference)

Your locked 5 pillars - every idea must fit one:

1. **Experiments** - Hands-on AI experiments, tools, prompts, demos, measurable outcomes
2. **Mental Models** - Complex concepts simplified, intuition, analogies, diagrams, frameworks
3. **Systems & Architecture** - How real AI systems built, pipelines, tradeoffs, infra, product decisions
4. **Learning Notes** - Clean summaries of papers/lessons, misconceptions, study clarity
5. **Meta/Process** - How learning happens, creator systems, productivity, public building (minimal)

---

## Brand Voice Checklist

When writing, ensure:

- [ ] **Curious** - Questions, experiments, learning journey visible
- [ ] **Analytical** - Numbers, frameworks, systems thinking
- [ ] **Calm confidence** - Authoritative but not arrogant
- [ ] **Builder mindset** - Practical, how-to, real problems
- [ ] **Educational, not sensational** - Accurate > viral
- [ ] **Honest** - Admit unknowns, show failures, learning public
- [ ] **Clear and concise** - Jargon minimized, concepts explained
- [ ] **Engineer-to-engineer** - Respect audience intelligence
- [ ] **Practical over theoretical** - Real examples beat abstraction
- [ ] **Smart but accessible** - Experts and learners both get value

## Quick Reference: Post Creation Framework

Before every post:

1. **Topic** - Clear, one idea
2. **Hook** - Surprise, clarify, or opportunity
3. **Core teaching point** - The insight
4. **Example / proof** - Concrete (code, diagram, data)
5. **CTA** - Save, share, follow, comment, or DM

## Success Metrics (Weekly)

| Metric | Target | What It Means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Saves rate | 5%+ | Content resonates, learnable |
| Shares rate | 1-2% | So useful people share |
| Follow rate | 0.5-1% per 1k | Audience wants more |
| Retention | 70%+ to end | Hook and pacing work |
| Comments | 10%+ engagement rate | Community cares |

## Next Steps After Each Post

2. **Engage** - Reply to all comments thoughtfully
3. **Analyze** - Check insights at 24h, 7d
4. **Document** - Note what worked: hook type, format, topic
5. **Iterate** - Apply to next week's priorities
