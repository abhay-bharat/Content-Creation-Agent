# Trending Topics & Idea Sources

Reference for discovering trending AI/ML/engineering topics aligned with AIThinkingLab audience.

## Daily Trend Sources

### X (Twitter)
- **Search**: [#AI](https://twitter.com/search?q=%23AI), [#MachineLearning](https://twitter.com/search?q=%23MachineLearning), [#ArtificialIntelligence](https://twitter.com/search?q=%23ArtificialIntelligence)
- **Follow**: Top AI researchers, engineers, tool builders in your niche
- **Best for**: Breaking news, opinions from experts, community discourse
- **Frequency**: Daily 10-15 min scan
- **Quality filter**: Look for threads with >100 quotes/retweets = validation

### Hacker News
- **Site**: https://news.ycombinator.com/
- **Keywords**: "AI", "machine learning", "LLM", "training"
- **Best for**: Technical depth, engineer perspective, product discussions
- **Frequency**: Daily morning
- **Quality filter**: >200 points + 50+ comments = strong community interest

### Reddit
- **Subreddits**: 
  - r/MachineLearning (papers, research)
  - r/LanguageModels (LLM-specific)
  - r/LocalLLaMA (open source, self-hosted)
  - r/ArtificialIntelligence (broader audience)
- **Best for**: Practitioner insights, pain points, experiments
- **Frequency**: 2-3x weekly
- **Quality filter**: >500 upvotes + lots of comments = resonates

## Weekly Trend Sources

### Research Papers
- **arXiv.org**
  - Top AI papers: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent

  - Focus: Papers with code (indicates real usage)
- **Browse**: New papers in your topic pillars
- **Best for**: Systems thinking, research-to-practice insights
- **Frequency**: Weekly scan (1-2 hours)

## Content Idea Generation Formulas

Combine these trends with your engineer lens:

### Formula 1: News + Misconception Correction
**Trend**: "New Claude model released"  
**Your take**: "What Claude really excels at (not what everyone thinks)"  
**Format**: Reel or Carousel  
**Hook**: Clarify

### Formula 2: Tool/Model + Practical Application
**Trend**: "Llama 3 open-sourced"  
**Your take**: "How to actually deploy this locally (with real tradeoffs)"  
**Format**: Carousel or Build Log  
**Hook**: Opportunity

### Formula 3: Paper + Engineer Intuition
**Trend**: "New attention mechanism paper"  
**Your take**: "Why this matters for your ML pipeline"  
**Format**: Mental Model Reel or Carousel  
**Hook**: Surprise

### Formula 4: Industry Problem + Solution Pattern
**Trend**: "RAG systems failing at scale"  
**Your take**: "The architecture pattern that fixes this"  
**Format**: Systems Thinking Carousel  
**Hook**: Opportunity

### Formula 5: Misconception + Correction
**Trend**: "Everyone talking about AI safety"  
**Your take**: "3 common misconceptions about alignment"  
**Format**: Carousel  
**Hook**: Clarify

## Red Flags: Topics to Avoid

These trends exist but don't fit AIThinkingLab:

- Generic "AI will change everything" predictions
- Fearbait / doomscenarios without substance
- Unverified income claims ("Make $10k with AI")
- Oversimplified tool tutorials without insight
- Pure hype / no engineering substance
- Celebrity drama unrelated to technical progress
- Outrage cycles

Instead: Extract the *technical* insight beneath the hype.

## Idea Validation Checklist

For each trending topic before adding to your idea list:

- [ ] Helps engineers think better?
- [ ] Technical accuracy possible?
- [ ] Your unique angle (not rehashing others)?
- [ ] Fits one of your 5 pillars?
- [ ] Audience would save/share?
- [ ] Can you create in 1-3 hours?
- [ ] Not just hype; lasting insight?

If YES to all → Add to weekly ideas  
If NO to any → Discard or rethink angle

## Weekly Trend Hunting Process

1. **Monday AM** (15 min): Scan X, HN, Reddit for breaking news
2. **Wednesday PM** (20 min): Check company blogs, arXiv papers
3. **Friday AM** (10 min): Spot-check Reddit, pick 2-3 ideas for next week
4. **Add to idea capture**: Populate weekly template with 5-10 new ideas

This keeps you on top of trends while respecting your time budget.
